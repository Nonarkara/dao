# Sources And Credits

## Core Text

The project uses the Wang Bi received text of the Dao De Jing as the Chinese base text.

English and Thai renderings are authored/adapted for this reading project by Non Arkaraprasertkul.

## C. C. Tsai

The Tsai illustration layer is inspired by:

- C. C. Tsai, adapted and illustrated, *Dao De Jing: The Illustrated Library of Chinese Classics*, translated by Brian Bruya, Princeton University Press, 2020.
- C. C. Tsai, *The Way of Nature* / Zhuangzi materials used only as Daoist parallels where appropriate.

Tsai's work has helped readers approach Chinese classics for more than fifty years. This site uses selected visual material as a credited study aid because the comics often explain a philosophical move faster than a paragraph can.

## Slingerland

Edward Slingerland is central to the modern framing of this site:

- *Trying Not to Try*
- work on wu-wei, de, embodied cognition, spontaneity, and early Chinese thought

The site treats Slingerland as the best modern doorway, not as a replacement for the Chinese text.
